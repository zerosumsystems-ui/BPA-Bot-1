## Core Trading Rules (from Intro slides 1-9)

### Trade Entry & Management
- Enter most trades using **stop orders** (not limit orders)
- Wait for a **strong signal bar** to increase probability of profit
- When **buying**: wait for a bull bar closing near its high, buy above that bar
- When **selling**: wait for a bear bar closing near its low, sell below that bar
- Do NOT simply enter above/below the first bar with a bull/bear body

### Stop Placement
- Place protective stop **just beyond the reversal point**
- Buy signal bar is the low → stop below that bar
- If signal bar is the 2nd+ bar of a multi-bar reversal and the 1st bar has a lower low → stop below the lowest bar
- Sell signals: initial stop above top of reversal or above major Lower High (LH)
- Buy signals: initial stop below bottom of reversal or below major Higher Low (HL)

### Risk Management (Critical Rule)
- **NEVER risk more than 3% of account on any single trade**
- $10,000 account → max risk $300 per trade
- This applies even when already profitable in a trade
- If profitable but stop is too far away (risk > 3%), reduce position size or exit
- Look to re-enter on a pullback where risk is again < 3%

### Trading Frequency
- **Beginners**: focus on swing trades only, 1-3 trades per day
- **Consistently profitable traders**: 3-10 trades per day, can add scalps

## Detailed Slide Learnings

### Basics: Be Flexible and Open to Anything (Slides 9-13)

#### Swing Trades vs Scalps (Slide 10)
**Successful Swing Trade:**
- Can be brief as a single big bar, but typically at least 2 legs and 10 bars
- Reward usually 20%+ of day's range and at least 3x average bar's range
- Reward at least twice as big as a scalp and at least twice risk
- Better to enter with a stop order
- Wait for major reversal or strong breakout
- Probability at least 40% (don't trade against strong trend)
- Most fail and result in small profit or loss

**Successful Scalp Trade:**
- Reward typically less than height of average bar; risk usually 1-2x as big as reward
- Need >70% probability to overcome bad risk/reward
- Reward is about 5-10% of average daily range
- Usually lasts 1-5 bars, might have 1-2-bar small pullback
- Can increase probability with wide stops and scaling in
- Limit order entry about half of the time
- Do NOT scalp against strong trend
- Often begins as swing, but scalp out once disappointed

#### 3 Building Blocks for All Trading (Slide 11)
Every pattern is built from these 3 foundations:
1. **Wedges** — 3-push patterns (bottom: 3 pushes down; top: 3 pushes up)
2. **Doubles** — Double Bottom / Double Top (2nd low/high can be at, above, or below 1st)
3. **Breakouts** — Consecutive big bars with small tails closing near extremes, above/below TR and EMA

**Bull versions:** Wedge Bottom, Double Bottom, Bull Breakout
**Bear versions:** Wedge Top, Double Top, Bear Breakout

#### Triple Bottoms & Tops: Variations, Rarely Perfect (Slides 12-13)
- **Perfect patterns are rare** — many things are variations of perfect patterns
- Any 3-push pattern can be thought of as a variation of a Triple Bottom/Top
- Variations include: Head & Shoulders (truncated wedge), Failed BO below DB/above DT, Wedge, Double Bottom/Top Pullback, Triangle
- **Key principle:** "If the market tries 2 or 3 times to go one way and fails, it often then goes the other way"

### Always In Long (AIL) — Detailed (Slides 14-28)

#### Core AIL Concept (Slide 15)
- **Always In Long** = traders expect higher prices and will buy
- **Two modes of AIL:**
  1. **Buy The Close** (strongest) — Tight Bull Channel; traders buy at the high of the trend; buy as soon as bull bar closes near its high or buy on stop above high of bar
  2. **Buy Pullback** (still bullish but weaker) — more bear bars and sideways trading; wait for pullback, then buy above bull bar closing near its high
- Consecutive bull bars with at least one big one closing near its high = AIL and Buy The Close

#### Consecutive Bull Bars = AIL (Slides 16-17)
- Consecutive bull bars on the open with one being big and closing near its high → Always In Long
- Buy above High of 2nd bar
- **CRITICAL: 50% of strong moves on the open reverse** — always use stops

#### One Exceptionally Big Bull Bar (Slide 18)
- When bull bar is **exceptionally big** compared to other bars → **70% chance of at least Measured Move up**
- Especially if follow-through bar does not have bear body
- Can also be bull leg in TR day; bear trend unlikely
- Sometimes one big bull bar is enough
- Traders usually want next bar to NOT have a bear body (confirmation)
- Entry options: Buy the Close, Buy 50% PB, Buy above PB

#### Big Gap Down but AIL on 1st Bar (Slide 19)
- Even after big gap down, if 1st bar is surprisingly big bull bar → AIL
- Buy above bar, or buy close of bar or close of bull follow-through bar
- Most traders confirm AIL once follow-through bar has bull body

#### Surprise Bar (Slides 20-21)
- **Surprise Bar** = extremely big bar compared to surrounding bars (low probability event)
- **60-70% chance of Measured Move up** based on body
- At least 2 more legs up, usually to a Wedge Top
- After huge bull surprise bar, even if strong selloff follows → likely TR, not bear trend
- Can buy 50% PB with limit order or on reversal up
- Became bull flag in Spike and Channel bull trend day

#### AIL: Bulls Buy above Flags (Slide 22)
- In strong AIL (Small PB Bull Trend): Buy High 1, High 2, and wedge bull flags
- Expect only 1-3 bar pullbacks
- When PB or pause (inside bar), buy above bull bar closing near its high
- If AIL bulls exit but still AIL, they buy again above bull bar in PB
- **Stop management:** Trail up to below most recent major Higher Low (bottom of most recent leg up) after every strong bull BO

#### Parabolic Wedge Bottom → LL MTR → AIL (Slide 23)
- Parabolic wedge down (3 legs) → strong minor reversal → Lower Low → MTR
- AIL after just 3 bars if context is good (weak selloff reversing from new low) and all 3 bars close near highs

#### If Exit but Still AIL: Buy Again (Slide 24)
- After parabolic wedge top (possible High of Day), okay to exit below bear bar
- If bar closes above midpoint with bull body → still AIL
- If exited, Always In Bulls buy again above bull bar
- Gap up with weak rally (no consecutive big bull bars) → usually enters tight TR for several hours

### Always In Short (AIS) — Detailed (Slides 29-42)

#### Core AIS Concept (Mirror of AIL)
- **Always In Short** = traders expect lower prices and will sell
- **Two modes of AIS:**
  1. **Sell The Close** (strongest) — Tight Bear Channel; traders sell at the low of the trend; sell as soon as bear bar closes near its low
  2. **Sell Pullback** (still bearish but weaker) — more bull bars and sideways trading; wait for pullback, then sell below bear bar closing near its low
- Consecutive bear bars with at least one big one closing near its low = AIS and Sell The Close

#### AIS Key Points
- Mirror of all AIL concepts applied to short side
- Consecutive bear bars on the open with one being big → Always In Short
- Sell below Low of 2nd bar; 50% of strong moves on open reverse → always use stops
- One exceptionally big bear bar → 70% chance of Measured Move down
- Surprise bear bar = 60-70% chance of MM down, at least 2 more legs down to wedge bottom
- After huge bear surprise bar, even if strong rally follows → likely TR, not bull trend
- In strong AIS (Small PB Bear Trend): Sell Low 1, Low 2, and wedge bear flags
- 3 consecutive bull bars in AIS context were bear flag (misleading counter-trend bars)
- **Stop management:** Trail down to above most recent major Lower High after every strong bear BO

### All-Time High Buy Climax (ATH BX) (Slides 43-47)

#### Parabolic Wedge at ATH
- When market makes ATH in parabolic wedge pattern → extreme buy climax
- Multi-timeframe analysis: what looks like a wedge on daily may be a broader pattern on weekly
- ATH buy climax often leads to significant reversal
- Look for exhaustion signs: big bull bars with large tails on top, consecutive buy climax bars
- After ATH BX: expect at least 2 legs down and 10+ bars of correction
- Reversal may form Double Top or Lower High before sustained move down

### Bankruptcy Risk / Trader's Equation (Slides 48-53)

#### The Trader's Equation
- **Core formula:** Probability × Reward vs. (1 - Probability) × Risk
- Trade is valid if: (Prob × Reward) > ((1-Prob) × Risk)
- Even low probability trades can be reasonable if reward is large enough relative to risk
- A 40% probability trade works if reward is at least 2x the risk
- A 60% probability scalp works if reward is at least 0.7x the risk

#### Bankruptcy Prevention
- Never risk more than 3% of account per trade (reinforced from intro)
- Consecutive losses happen to all traders — position sizing prevents ruin
- Beginners should trade small until consistently profitable
- The math of recovery: losing 50% requires 100% gain to break even

### Bar Counting (Slides 54-57+)

#### High 1, High 2, High 3 (H1, H2, H3) — Bull Flags
- **H1 (High 1):** Once a bar goes above the prior bar in a pullback, that pullback becomes an H1 buy signal setup (H1 bull flag)
- **H2 (High 2):** If 1st move up fails and there is a 2nd entry, the pullback is an H2 bull flag
  - "If bears twice try to selloff and fail, they will give up" → Creates H2 Bull Flag
  - Buy above bull bar, since bears exiting with loss
  - **Every Double Bottom is an H2**
- **H3:** = triangle or wedge pattern (3rd attempt)
- H1, H2, and H3 are pullbacks in bull trend or Trading Range
- **Beginners should look for strong bull trend** for these setups
- In past 15 bars, better if no more than 1 close below EMA
- Higher probability of profit when buying above bull bar that closes near its high

#### Bull Flag Variations, Rarely Perfect (Slide 56)
- Perfect bull flag patterns are rare in real trading
- Most real-world flags have imperfect shapes
- Traders must learn to recognize variations of the ideal pattern

#### Low 1, Low 2, Low 3 (L1, L2, L3) — Bear Flags (Slide 60)
- Mirror of H1/H2/H3 applied to bear side
- **L1:** Once a bar goes below prior bar in a pullback, that pullback becomes an L1 sell signal setup (L1 bear flag)
- **L2:** If 1st move down fails and there is a 2nd entry → L2 bear flag
  - "If bulls twice try to rally and fail, they will give up" → Creates L2 Bear Flag
  - **Every Double Top is an L2**
- **L3** = triangle or wedge pattern (3rd attempt)
- L1, L2, L3 are pullbacks in bear trend or TR
- Beginners should look for strong bear trend
- In past 15 bars, better if no more than 1 close above EMA

#### H3/L3 Classification (Slides 58-59, 61)
- **H3 tilted down** = Wedge bull flag; **H3 horizontal** = Triangle bull flag
- **L3 tilted up** = Wedge bear flag; **L3 horizontal** = Triangle bear flag
- Does not matter what you call it — rarely perfect
- Need either 3 reversals or 3 legs in the flag

#### Consecutive Complex Patterns (Slides 62-65)
- **Consecutive Complex Bottoms (H4/H5):** Wedge bull flag (H3) + H2 (LL DB) → Two Legs Up Likely
- **Consecutive Low 2 Tops (L4 Top):** Wedge LH + OO pattern → micro double top + 2nd S signal for wedge
- **Consecutive Tops (Wedge then L2):** Start counting after BO begins trend. Wedge (3 pushes) + L2 = L4 variant
- **Low 4: TBTL (Ten Bars, Two Legs)** — Wedge top and L4 top both expect TBTL sideways to down
  - Expect about half as many bars as in the rally and expect 2 clear legs

### Bar Numbers — 5-min Charts (Slides 66-72)

#### Odds of New H or L: Decrease Later in Day (Slide 67)
**Critical statistical data for 5-min ES charts:**
- Bar 1: 20-30% chance of being HOD or LOD
- Depends on context (gap, TR, trend from yesterday) and strength of 1st bar
- **By close of Bar 7: 50% chance** you've seen either HOD or LOD
- **By Bar 12: 70%**
- **By Bar 18: 80-90%** (1.5 hours into session, 8:00 am PT)
- **By Bar 80: 99.99%** (81 bars in the day)

#### Big Bear Bar on 1st Bar (Slide 68)
- Big bear bar closing on its low on 1st bar → 30% chance it becomes HOD
- Context matters: big gap up that reversed down = good for bears

#### 18-Bar Range BO Rules (Slides 69-70)
- **BO below 1st 18 bars → probably no new High today**
- When 18-bar range is **big**: only **10% chance** of opposite BO
- When 18-bar range is **small**: **20% chance** of BO on both sides
- **90% rule:** Strong BO to new L after bar 18 → 90% chance H remains HOD
- Strong BO to new H after bar 18 → 90% chance L remains LOD

#### Midday Reversals (Slides 71-72)
- Midday reversals typically happen around **Bars 39-42** on 5-min charts
- Bar 39-41 = middle of trading day
- Wedge top at Bar 39 → reversal down; Wedge bottom at Bar 41 → reversal up

- Big Down + Big Up = **Big Confusion** = Trading Range likely

- 1st BDBU can fail to create TR → trend continues
- BDBU with close near open → doji, confirms TR day
- Big confusion and uncertainty = both sides have reasons to trade their way

### Additional 18-Bar Range BO Details (Slides 145-216)

#### Failed BO Reversal Rules
- **Failed BO usually reverses within 1st 3 bars** of breaking out

- Consecutive big bull bars closing near highs = strong BO

### Breakout and Channel Bull Trend / Spike and Channel Bull Trend (Slides 217-272)
*Section 25 — massive section with 60+ subsections, ~100+ slides*

#### Core Spike and Channel Concept (Slides 217-225)
- **Spike and Channel = two-phase trend pattern:**
  1. **Spike phase:** Strong BO (surprise bar, consecutive trend bars, big gap)
  2. **Channel phase:** Weaker continuation trend after the spike
- Body gaps in spike = strong trend (measuring gaps)
- **MM (Measured Move):** Based on channel height, projected from BO point
- Spike and Channel eventually evolves into TR or reverses
- Channel often ends with wedge pattern (3 pushes)

#### Breakout and Channel Variations (Slides 225-240)
- **Failed Bull BO and Channel** (Section 27): BO fails → reversal
- **Failed BO reverses within first 3 bars** of breaking out
- **Cup and Handle** (Section 25.14): Rounded bottom = bullish continuation
- **BO and Channel: Then TR** (Section 25.15): Channel eventually becomes TR
- **Bull Channel: Bull BO and MM Up** (Section 25.16): Channel can break out further
- **Trending Trading Ranges** (Slide 237): Series of TRs with HLs and HHs = also a bull channel
- **Small PB Bull Trend as Channel** (Slide 236): Channel phase of BO & Channel can be Small PB Bull Trend; body gaps = measuring gaps; exhaustive buy climax at MM target

#### Spike and Channel Key Details (Slides 238-272)
- **ii Top ending** (Slide 235): S&C trends can end with inside-inside bar at buy climax → EOD profit taking
- **No Close Below EMA** (Slide 238):
  - **99.5% of days have at least 3 bars with lows below EMA**
  - **99.5% of all days have one bear bar closing below EMA**
  - ~1 day per year has no bear bar closing below EMA (extremely rare)
  - 20-Gap Bar Buy = triangle pattern in strong trend
- **Tight Bull Channel** (Slide 239): In tight channel, only buy — never sell
  - Midday BO from TR = spike, then channel follows
- **Bull Channel Trading** (Slide 252):
  - Limit order bears made money selling at/above prior high
  - Limit order bulls made money buying around EMA
  - Both sides can profit in channel phase
- **After Major Bull Surprise Bar** (Slide 263): At least **40% chance of bull trend day**
  - Need wide stop → trade smaller position size
  - Minimum goal = MM up based on body size
- **Not Small PB Bull Trend vs Bull Channel** (Slide 271): Critical distinction:
  - Small PB Bull Trend: body gaps persist (measuring gaps) → very strong
  - Bull Channel: body gaps keep closing → not as strong, but still bullish
  - Strong selloffs at EMA look like reversals but are bear traps (DT Bull Flags)
  - Never becomes AIS despite 3 bear bars and good sell signals
  - No closes far below EMA = stays AIL
  - **Do not sell at bottom of 3 bear bars at EMA**
- **Nested S&C Patterns** (Slide 272): Fractal concept — smaller Spike and Channel patterns nest within larger S&C patterns; truncated wedges and LH DT at tops

#### Spike and Channel Summary Rules
1. Two phases: Strong spike → weaker channel
2. Body gaps = strong trend (measuring gaps in Small PB trend)
3. Body gaps closing = weaker (bull channel, not Small PB)
4. Channel typically ends with wedge (3 pushes)
5. MM target based on channel height
6. Only buy in tight bull channel (never sell)
7. Limit order traders can trade both sides in wider channels
8. S&C patterns are fractal — they nest within each other
9. 40%+ chance of trend day after major surprise bar
10. 99.5% of days have at least one bear bar closing below EMA

### Failed Bull Breakout / Bull Traps (Slides 317-341)
*Section 27 — 24 subsections covering failed bull BOs and trap concepts*

#### Bull Trap Core Concept (Slide 318/27.1)
- **"Trap simply means you discovered you are now doing the wrong thing"**
- Trap is often **both bull and bear trap** simultaneously — trapping one side out AND other side in
- **Three types of bull traps:**
  1. **Into Bad Long (Bull Surprise failed):** Big bull bar closing near H and far above EMA → next bar reverses down. If bull bought the close, trapped into bad long. Still AIS.

#### Bear Trends Relative Strength Spectrum (KEY — Slide 538, 34.1)
**Six levels of bear trend strength, from strongest to weakest:**

2. **Bear Micro Channel** — Very strong. Small bodies, mostly bear. Tiny pullbacks (1-bar, small). Still very strong bears.

### Section 2: High 1 Bull Flag

**Core Concept**: High 1 = first pullback in bull trend where bar's high is at or above prior bar's high. Simplest bull flag.

**Key Rules**:
- High 1 in strong bull trend = buy above the signal bar
- High 1 is a pause in bull trend, PB or 1-bar pullback
- Multiple High 1 setups = very strong Small PB Bull Trend

### Section 3: High 2 and 3 Bull Flags and Bottoms

**Core Concept**: High 2 = 2nd pullback buy signal; High 3 = 3rd attempt.

**Key Rules**:
- High 2 is more reliable than High 1 (2nd attempt to reverse failed)
- High 3 in broad bull channel = strong buy
- High 2/3 at EMA = high probability buy setup

### Section 4: High 4 Bull Flags and Bottoms

**Core Concept**: High 4 = 4th buy attempt, often last chance for bulls.

**Key Rules**:
- High 4 = BO below High 2, then another High 2 pattern
- After High 4, if fails → likely bear trend
- High 4 at bottom of TR = strong buy

### Section 5: Head and Shoulders Bottom (19+ subsections)

**Core Concept**: Classic H&S bottom reversal pattern with Brooks PA interpretation.

**Key Rules**:
- H&S Bottom = 3 push down pattern (left shoulder, head, right shoulder)
- Expanding Triangle Bottom = variant of H&S
- Wedge Bottom: PB from 1st Leg Up is Right shoulder
- 2nd Failed BO below = strong buy signal
- B above Strong Bull Bar at neckline

### Section 6: Failed Head and Shoulders Bottom

**Core Concept**: When H&S bottom fails, bears regain control.

**Key Rules**:
- Failed BO above DT = failed BO above neckline of H&S Bottom
- Failed H&S Bottom 2nd Attempt = even weaker bulls
- Wedge Bottom: Sell Climax after failed H&S

### Section 7: Head and Shoulders Top

**Core Concept**: H&S top reversal pattern — 3 push up.

**Key Rules**:
- Classic H&S top = left shoulder rally, head (highest), right shoulder (lower high)
- Neckline break = sell signal
- Right shoulder often is wedge rally

### Section 8: Failed Head and Shoulders Top

**Core Concept**: When H&S top fails, bulls resume strongly.

**Key Rules**:
- Failed H&S top = trapped bears, strong bull continuation
- Buy above failed right shoulder breakdown

### Section 9: HTF (Higher Time Frame) B and S Signals

## Section 2: MA 20-Gap Bar Buy (60+ subsections)

**Core Concept**: 20-Gap Bar = bar with low above the 20-bar EMA (gap between bar and EMA). Consecutive gap bars indicate strong trend.

**Key Rules**:
- **50 bars without pullback to EMA = 99.5% chance of at least 3 bars with low below EMA**
- 20-Gap Bar Buy: Strong bull trend where every bar's low is above EMA
- Eventually must pull back — the longer the gap, the more certain the pullback
- Gap bars can act as measuring gaps in strong trends
- Consecutive gap bars = strong momentum, but exhaustion inevitable

## Section 4: MA 20-Gap Bar Sell (multiple subsections)

**Key Rules**:

- Eventually exhausts into pullback or reversal

## Part 11: Classifications N to P (695 slides)

### Sections 2-10: Nested Pattern, Next X Points, Open Patterns

**Nested Pattern (Sections 2-4)**
- Nested pattern = pattern within a pattern (e.g., ii inside a larger wedge)
- Important because it compounds probability — when nested setups align, confidence increases
- Look for nested patterns at key support/resistance levels for highest probability trades

**Next X Points (Sections 5-7)**
- Focus on "what are the next 10 points likely to do?" rather than predicting exact tops/bottoms
- Helps frame trades in terms of immediate directional probability
- Especially useful in trading ranges where breakout direction is uncertain

**Open Was High/Low of Day (Sections 8-9)**
- Open = HOD: bearish implication, market opened and immediately sold off
- Open = LOD: bullish implication, market opened and immediately rallied
- Both are statistically significant patterns that indicate strong directional conviction from open

**Failed Open = HOD/LOD (Section 10)**
- When Open = HOD fails (market rallies above open), expect strong bull move
- When Open = LOD fails (market drops below open), expect strong bear move
- Failed patterns often lead to measured moves in the opposite direction

### Sections 11-15: Outside Down Patterns

**Outside Down Day/Bar (Section 11)**
- Outside Down = bar with higher high AND lower low than prior bar, closing near low
- TR Open lasting half the day with range less than half the average daily range = expect BO and MM
- Key principle: small range early in day compressing = energy building for breakout
- Outside down bars at resistance are strong sell signals
- Multiple outside down bars in sequence = increasing bear momentum

**Outside Down Day Almost Formed (Section 12)**
- When an outside down day almost forms but fails = potential bull trap for bears
- Bears who sold expecting outside down completion get trapped
- Watch for reversal back up when outside down pattern fails to complete
- The "almost" pattern is itself tradeable — failed bear setups become bull setups

### Sections 16-22: Consecutive Outside Bars, Bull Flag, OO Patterns

**Consecutive Outside Bars / OO Pattern (Section 16)**
- OO (Outside-Outside) = two consecutive outside bars
- **KEY INSIGHT: OO = Expanding Triangle on smaller timeframe**
- This means OO represents increasing volatility and indecision
- OO is inherently a Breakout Mode pattern — buy above, sell below
- Bull Flag can contain OO patterns as part of the consolidation

**OO Bottom Patterns (Sections 18-22)**
- OO at a bottom = Breakout Mode setup
- **OO is BO Mode: buy above the high, sell below the low**
- At bottoms, the buy above signal has higher probability
- Look for OO at support levels, prior lows, or after measured moves down
- OO bottom with bull reversal bar on 2nd outside bar = stronger buy signal

### Sections 23-29: OO Top Patterns

**OO Top (Sections 23-29)**
- **OO Top: expect 2 legs down** — this is a key probabilistic rule
- OO at a top after extended bull move = high probability reversal setup
- First leg down from OO top often reaches EMA
- Second leg tests or exceeds the first leg's low
- OO top with bear reversal bar on 2nd outside bar = stronger sell signal
- Even if market reverses back up after 2 legs, the 2-leg expectation is reliable for scalps

### Sections 30-32: Outside Up Day

**Outside Up Day (Section 30, 18+ subsections)**
- Outside Up = bar with higher high AND lower low, closing near high
- Bullish pattern, especially after a sell-off or at support
- Outside up day after a bear trend = potential major reversal signal
- The more bars the outside up bar engulfs, the stronger the signal
- Outside up bars in a bull trend = continuation and acceleration
- At bottoms: outside up bar after multiple bear bars = trapped bears

### Section 33: Outside Up Bar - Major Bull Surprise

**Major Bull Surprise Outside Up Bar (Section 33)**
- When an outside up bar is a major bull surprise = very strong signal
- Major surprise means the bar was unexpected given prior context
- These bars often lead to measured moves up
- Gaps up that create outside up bars are especially powerful
- Follow-through after major bull surprise outside up is typically strong
- Look for pullback entry if you miss the initial move

### Sections 35-40: Probability and Pullback Patterns

**Probability Concepts (Sections 35-37)**
- Every setup has a probability, reward, and risk component
- High probability trades can have small rewards (scalps)
- Low probability trades need larger rewards to be profitable
- Trader's equation: Probability × Reward > (1 - Probability) × Risk
- Focus on setups where the math works in your favor

**Pullback Patterns (Sections 38-40)**
- Pullbacks to 5%, 10%, 20%, 50% levels are common measured move targets
- Deeper pullbacks (20%+) increase probability of trend reversal
- Shallow pullbacks (5-10%) in strong trends = continuation likely
- 50% pullback of any move is a key decision point
- Pullback depth helps determine whether trend or trading range is more likely

### Section 41: PB Below Bottom of Big Bull Bar

**Pullback Below Big Bull Bar (Section 41)**
- **KEY INSIGHT: Bull trend can begin before the final bear low**
- A pullback below the bottom of a big bull bar can be a bear trap
- Bears see new low and sell, but the trend has already reversed
- This is why waiting for "confirmation" can cause you to miss entries
- The big bull bar itself is the signal — the pullback below is the trap
- Look for strong rejection (long tail, bull close) after the pullback

### Section 45: Parabolic Wedge Top with Endless Pullback

**Parabolic Wedge Top + Endless PB (Section 45)**
- Parabolic move = accelerating trend with increasingly steep legs
- Wedge top = 3 pushes up with decreasing momentum
- **Endless PB = major reversal possible even from tight channel**
- When a parabolic wedge top is followed by an endless pullback, the reversal is significant
- Tight bull channels can still lead to major reversals when parabolic exhaustion occurs
- The "endless" nature means the pullback keeps going — what looks like a small correction becomes a trend reversal
- Key warning sign: 3 pushes up with each push having less momentum + first pullback that doesn't bounce

### Sections 51-54: Buying Pressure and Selling Pressure

**Buying Pressure (Section 51)**
- Buying pressure = evidence that bulls are actively defending and pushing price up
- Look for: bull bars closing near highs, long tails below bars, failed bear breakouts
- Consecutive bull bars with closes above midpoints = sustained buying pressure
- Buying pressure in a bear trend = potential reversal building
- 3+ consecutive bull bars with good closes = strong buying pressure signal

**Selling Pressure (Sections 53-54)**
- **3+ legs with big bear bars closing near lows = sideways to down likely**
- Selling pressure = evidence that bears are actively pushing price down
- Look for: bear bars closing near lows, long tails above bars, failed bull breakouts
- Selling pressure in a bull trend = potential reversal or trading range
- Failed selling pressure (3+ bear bars that fail to follow through) = bull signal
- When selling pressure fails, trapped bears fuel the next leg up

## Part 12: Classifications Q to SL (373 slides)

### Sections 2-5: Reversal Down, Failed Reversals, Many Abrupt Reversals

**Reversal Down (Section 2)**
- Reversal down patterns include wedge tops, double tops, head and shoulders tops
- Bear reversal day with wedge top at EMA after big gap down = strong sell setup
- Around 50% PB from yesterday's close is common reversal point
- Sell below bear bar closing near its low

**Reversal Down Failed and Bull Trend Resumed (Section 3)**
- When reversal down fails, it becomes a bull flag
- Failed bear setups trap bears and fuel strong bull moves
- Bull trend resumption after failed reversal often leads to measured move up

**Reversal Down and then Reversal Up (Section 4)**
- Markets can reverse down, then reverse up — creating a trading range
- Look for the second reversal for higher probability entries
- The first reversal tests support, the second confirms it

**Many Abrupt Reversals (Section 5)**
- In 3-day TRs, expect many abrupt reversals
- Many abrupt reversals without 2nd signals = wait for confirmation
- 9 consecutive bear bars at low of TR = buy above 2nd consecutive bull bar closing near high
- **KEY: In TR with many abrupt reversals, always wait for 2nd signals before entering**

### Sections 6-8: Reversal Up, Failed Reversals

**Reversal Up (Section 6)**
- Bull trend reversal after deep selloff: rally typically doesn't go far above earlier high
- Wedge bottom + micro DB = buy above bull bar closing near its high
- If stopped out of 1st buy but new wedge forms, buy again above doji reversal bar or bull bar closing near high
- Bull reversal patterns include wedge bottoms, double bottoms, head and shoulders bottoms

**Reversal Up and then Reversal Down (Section 7)**
- Similar to Section 4 but from the bull side
- Creates trading range — look for failed reversal up as sell signal
- Important to recognize when reversal up is likely to fail

**Reversal Up Failed and Bear Trend Resumed (Section 8)**
- Failed cup and handle bottom = strong bear signal
- Major bear breakout after failed reversal up = bear trend resumption
- Pullback from strong reversal up after sell climax creates cup and handle bottom — when this fails, bears are in control

### Section 9: Reversing Position

**Reversing Position**
- Reversing = closing current position and entering opposite direction
- Only reverse when strong signal in opposite direction with good context
- Don't reverse in middle of TR — too likely to get whipsawed
- Best reversals: strong trend bar in new direction at key support/resistance

### Sections 10-12: Sell The Close, Signal Bar Context

**Sell The Close at End of Day (Section ~10)**
- Selling the close at end of day: risk happens fast
- Middle of TR day and test of open = risky sell location
- Bears who sell the close will exit and stop selling, so can get sharp rally
- **KEY: Sell the close bears exiting creates buying pressure for next day's open**

**Strong Buy Signal Bar: Bad Context (Section ~11)**
- Good buy signal bars in bad context (Tight Bear Channel) = likely to fail
- If you buy in tight bear channel, need wide stop and scaling in
- **Context overrides signal bar quality** — this is a fundamental Brooks principle
- Strong signal bar in wrong context = trap

**Sell Signal Bars: Bad Context (Section ~12)**
- Sell signal bars after big bull bars and sell climax = traps
- Sell signal never triggered because context was bullish (HL MTR, breakout test, bull channel)
- Bad context after sell climax = don't sell, look for buys instead

### Sections 13-15: Small Signal Bars, Signal Bar Size

**Small Buy Signal Bar: Use Wider Stop (Section ~13)**
- When buy signal bar is small, risk is small
- **Low risk means good risk/reward BUT low probability**
- Higher probability to use wider stop, like 4-6 ticks below bull bar
- Small signal bars at DB = use wider stop to increase probability
- Trade-off: small signal bar gives good R:R but needs wider stop for better probability

**Small Sell Signal Bar (Section ~14)**
- Same principle as small buy signal bar but in reverse
- Small sell signal bars have lower probability of triggering
- Use wider stop above the signal bar for higher probability short

### Sections 16-17: Strong Signal Bar, BO Test

**Strong Signal Bar (Section 16)**
- Strong signal bars have body closing near extreme (high for bull, low for bear)
- Strong signal bars in good context = high probability
- Even strong signal bars can fail in bad context
- Look for strong signal bars at support/resistance, after pullbacks, at trend line tests

**BO Test: Failed to Get Above Bottom of Bad Sell Signal Bar (Section ~17)**
- Bull bar in Tight Bull Channel = bad sell signal bar
- Some bulls buy below low of bad sell signal bar expecting pullback to go at least one tick above bottom
- They then buy more above next bull bar closing near high
- When rally stops one tick below bad sell signal bar = bulls trapped
- Bears short the bull trap below bear bar
- **KEY: Failed BO tests create trapped traders who fuel the move in opposite direction**

### Sections 2-3: Takeover and Time Frames

**Takeover**
- Bull takeover: consecutive bull bars closing near highs, strong BO signal
- Bear takeover: consecutive bear bars closing near lows
- Takeover signals shift in control between bulls and bears
- BO above bull channel: 25% of the time get BO above top of channel instead of below bottom
- Consecutive big bull bars closing near highs = strong BO above bull channel after failed BO below bull trend line

**Time Frames**
- Always look at higher time frame context for intraday trades
- What looks like a trend on 5-min may be a leg in TR on 60-min
- Higher time frame patterns override lower time frame signals

### Sections 4-8: Ticks and Market Crashes

**Ticks During Stock Market Crashes**
- Extreme tick readings (-1000 or below) indicate panic selling
- Ticks measure buying vs selling pressure across all NYSE stocks
- Crash conditions: sustained negative ticks with brief bounces

**Ticks Below -1,000 in Bear Trend**
- Ticks below -1,000 = extreme selling pressure across all stocks
- In strong bear trend, -1,000 ticks confirms broad market selling
- Can be used as confirmation for short entries

**Ticks Below -1,000 and Reversal Up Without Divergence**
- Sometimes market reverses up even without tick divergence
- Reversal happens because sellers are exhausted (all who wanted to sell have sold)
- Don't rely solely on tick divergence for reversal signals

**Ticks Reaching +1,000 During Bull Trend**
- Ticks above +1,000 = extreme buying pressure
- Confirms broad market participation in bull trend
- Can be exhaustion signal if late in trend

**Ticks Reaching +1,000 and Reversal Down Without Divergence**
- Market can reverse down even without tick divergence
- Buyers exhausted after extreme readings

### Sections 9-10: Trading Range Patterns and Traps

**TR: Lower Highs in TR Is Early Bear Channel**
- Bear trend = series of lower highs and lows
- Often begins with minor lower highs in TR
- LH in TR is warning of possible early bear channel
- Watch for the subtle transition from TR to bear trend

**Bear Flag: Failed Bull BO above Wedge Bear Flag**
- 2nd Leg Trap Up: B vacuum test of resistance at top of Broad Bear Channel and TR
- Strong BO above wedge bear flag → probable 2nd leg up in TR or broad bear channel
- Got deep PB so TR likely (not trend reversal)

**2nd Leg Bear Trap: Bear Trending Trading Range Day**
- Major Bear Surprise → bull trend unlikely
- Possible 2nd Leg Bear Trap at Measured Move and 60-min EMA
- When Major Bear Surprise fails (reverses up), usually a 2nd leg down and 2nd Leg Bear Trap
- Since Major Bear Surprise, minor reversal more likely than bull trend
- 2nd leg down often big enough to be Major Bear Surprise → expect TR

**Bull Trap: Midday Reversal**
- Bull trap = failed BO above wedge
- Minor wedge since earlier major bull BO
- Expanding triangle pattern
- Look for sell below bear bar closing on its low

**End Of Day Bull Trap: Then Bear Trap**
- BO above wedge rally = End Of Day Bull trap
- Sell below bear bar closing on its low, or wait for 2nd sell signal
- Big bear BO after bull trap = End Of Day Bear Trap
- Buy above bull bar closing on its high
- **KEY: End of day traps on both sides create whipsaw opportunities**

**Bear Trap: Then Small Pullback Bull Trend**
- Wedge at EMA with 2 bear traps → small PB bull trend
- Small PB bull trend always forms parabolic wedge tops but ONLY BUY
- Very small pullbacks in small PB bull trend = only buy
- 20-Gap bar buy signal in strong trends

**2nd Leg Bear Trap: Expect at Least Small 2nd Leg Down**
- 3 big bear bars closing near lows = Surprise BO
- BO below HL DB and wedge bull flag, but possible 2nd leg bear trap
- Strong enough selloff → at least small 2nd leg sideways to down before reversal up
- 2nd leg down = wedge bull flag, doji bar, tight TR
- **Higher probability to wait for bull BO before buying** (don't buy the 1st reversal attempt)

**Bear Trap Failed: Trend Resumption Down**
- Failed bear trap → bull trap → trend resumption down
- When traps fail, they create stronger moves in the original direction
- Failed traps = trapped traders fueling the move

### Sections 2-9: Wedge Bottom Patterns

**Wedge Bottom (Section 4)**
- Wedge bottom = 3 pushes down (3 legs), each making lower low
- Classic reversal pattern: buy above bull bar after 3rd push down
- Reversal up from wedge bottom usually has 2 legs up
- PB from 1st leg up usually is HL MTR (Higher Low Major Trend Reversal) and HSB (High Sell Bar)
- 2nd failed BO below yesterday's low = nested wedge bottom (stronger signal)

**Wedge Bottom, 2nd Buy Signal (Section 2)**
- If 1st buy signal from wedge bottom fails, 2nd buy signal is higher probability
- 2nd signal often forms HL MTR pattern
- Wait for 2nd entry if first one is unclear

**Wedge Bottom, Bad Shape (Section 3)**
- Wedge doesn't always look textbook perfect
- Can have imperfect spacing between legs
- Still valid if general 3-push structure is present
- Context matters more than perfect shape

**Wedge Bottom, but Bear BO or TR (Sections 5-6)**
- Sometimes wedge bottom leads to bear BO instead of reversal up
- Failed wedge bottom became breakout in breakout and channel bear trend → MM down
- When wedge bottom fails, expect at least MM down
- DT Bear Flag after failed wedge bottom = strong sell signal

**Wedge Bottom, but Bear BO Failed and Reversed Up (Section 7)**
- Failed BO below wedge = also creates new wedge (nested pattern)
- Bear Surprise = possible new 1st leg down
- Wedge with doji and 5 bear bars → failed BO below = new wedge forming
- Failed breakouts below wedge bottoms create strong reversal signals

**Wedge Bottom With Double Bottom (Sections 8-9)**
- Wedge + DB = stronger reversal signal (confluence)
- DB at 3rd leg of wedge = highest probability long entry
- Even strong confluences can fail → always use stops

### Sections 10-15: Parabolic Wedge Bottom

**Parabolic Wedge Bottom**
- Parabolic = accelerating selloff (each leg steeper than prior)
- Sell climax behavior: exhaustion after parabolic move
- Parabolic wedge bottom → small pullback bull trend (strong reversal)
- MM based on bodies of bull breakout bars
- Micro wedge at top = buy climax signal
- Bar 44 midday reversal pattern common after parabolic wedge reversal

### Sections 16-19: Wedge Top Patterns

**Wedge Top (Section 19-21)**
- Wedge top = 3 pushes up, each making higher high
- Often has 4-5 legs up (not always exactly 3)
- When wedge has strong 2nd leg up, many computer algorithms restart count
- Wedge channel often doesn't look quite right; can be tight
- Multiple ways to draw the wedge channel
- **KEY: When in doubt, stay out**
- Wait for: sell signal bar closing near its low, micro DT, 2nd S signal bar, or strong reversal down
- If S (sell) and get stopped out but premise still valid, look to sell again (consecutive tops)

**Wedge Top Failed (Section 20)**
- Failed BO above parabolic wedge = buy climax
- Big doji bar at top of buy climax: even though small body, big move from top to bottom = Bear Surprise
- Expect a couple legs sideways to down after buy climax
- Failed bull BO above wedge top → bear trend reversal day

### Sections 22-26: Parabolic Wedge Top

**Parabolic Wedge Top**
- Micro wedge top = small parabolic wedge (same pattern, smaller scale)
- Parabolic wedge: reversal down, then selloff and trading range
- Minor parabolic wedge top → then trading range
- Parabolic wedge top → then DT LH MTR (double top, lower high major trend reversal)

### Sections 27-30: Truncated Wedge Patterns

**Truncated Wedge Top**
- Truncated = 3rd push did not go above 2nd push (lower high)
- LH DT = truncated wedge top
- Truncated wedge is ALWAYS also a DT (double top)
- 2nd reversal down from above prior resistance = strong signal
- 3rd top was LH = even stronger sell signal
- Minor reversal down after truncated wedge → then often trading range

**Truncated Wedge Bottom**
- 3rd push did not go below 2nd push (higher low)
- Truncated wedge bottom is always also a DB (double bottom)
- Failed truncated wedge bottom → formed new wedge (nested pattern)

### Sections 31-35: Wedge Bull/Bear Flags

**Wedge Bull Flag**
- 3 legs down in a pullback during bull trend
- Buy signal above bull bar after 3rd leg down
- Wedge bull flag is a type of High 2 pattern (buy the 2nd pullback)

**Wedge Bear Flag**
- 3 legs up in a pullback during bear trend
- Sell signal below bear bar after 3rd leg up
- Wedge bear flag is a type of Low 2 pattern (sell the 2nd rally)
